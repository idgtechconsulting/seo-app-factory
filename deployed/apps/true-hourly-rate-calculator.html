Why this matters for freelancers

Most people calculate their hourly rate by dividing their salary by 2,080 hours (40 hours × 52 weeks). However, this is a mathematical illusion.

If you are a freelancer or contractor, you don't get paid for:

  • Public holidays (Christmas, New Year's, etc.)
  • Vacation time to prevent burnout
  • Sick days and personal emergencies
  • Administrative time (invoicing, searching for clients)

To maintain the same standard of living as a salaried employee, you must increase your hourly rate to cover these "non-billable" hours. This calculator helps you find that True Hourly Rate.
